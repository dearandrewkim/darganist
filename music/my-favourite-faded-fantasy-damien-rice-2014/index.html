About

Darganist is where you go when you're looking for something something new and want a humans help to find it. We write about the things that move us, frustrate us, and, not infrequently, cause us to be incapacitated by overwhelming joy.

We won't write half-heartedly about things that don't matter. We won't tell you what you already know. We won't agree with prevailing opinion because it's easy and safe to do so.

We'll simply direct your attention to something we've listened to, read or watched, and ask the crucial question: on a scale of one to nuclear holocaust, how likely is this to improve your day?

title:

My Favourite Faded Fantasy

artist: Damien Rice

year: 2014

rating: 3.75

Andrew

Finally. After 8 years of waiting since his last album came out, Damien Rice has produced yet another wonderfully depressing album.

For those of you who aren’t familiar with Damien Rice, he’s both a talented singer-songwriter and a hopeless romantic who understands all of your heartbreaks. His songs are dramatic, lyrical and incredibly (yet wonderfully) depressing. I promise that you’ll be wanting to cry whilst you listen to his achingly defeated voice sing over his guitar asÂ the violins and cellos weep through their harmonies and vibratos. And after you finished listening, you’ll realise that love is powerful yet cruel, but perhaps it’s precisely these emotions which defines us as humans.
