Darganist is where you go when you're looking for something something new and want a humans help to find it. We write about the things that move us, frustrate us, and, not infrequently, cause us to be incapacitated by overwhelming joy.

We won't write half-heartedly about things that don't matter. We won't tell you what you already know. We won't agree with prevailing opinion because it's easy and safe to do so.

We'll simply direct your attention to something we've listened to, read or watched, and ask the crucial question: on a scale of one to nuclear holocaust, how likely is this to improve your day?

title:

Burn Your Fire For No Witness

artist: Angel Olsen

year: 2014

rating: 3.5

Andrew

Angel Olsen is an American folk and indie rock singer, who must have lived through World War Two or War of the Worlds because her music is really depressing and filled with rage.

But this is precisely the reason why I love her music – she manages to express this anger and confusion in a poetic and lyrical way. The grungy electronic guitar strumming chords over a simple rock drum beats, over layed by Angel Olsen’s sweet folky voice singing into a lo-fi microphone, perfectly articulates her and her audiences’ heart broken memories.

Lights Out is a song which must be sung to people who complain and blame others.

if you don’t feel good about it then turn around if you really mean it baby stand your ground no one’s gonna take it for you, darling it’s true no one’s gonna wait there with you

Listen to this album when you’ve just experienced a breakup and need to express your frustration and confusion through a woman’s voice.
